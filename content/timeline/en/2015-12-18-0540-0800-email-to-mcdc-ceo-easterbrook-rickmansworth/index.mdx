## Direct Engagement and Acknowledgement of Receipt

On December 18, 2015, I sent another email to Steve Easterbrook after noticing a connection to my site [White-Collar-Criminals.com](https://www.White-Collar-Criminals.com) from Rickmansworth, near Easterbrook’s hometown of Watford. This connection indicated that Easterbrook might have most likely personally received and reviewed the accusations I laid out, reinforcing the urgency and personal engagement in the matter.

## Questioning Legal Advice and Inaction

In the email, I expressed concerns about the legal advice Easterbrook was receiving, specifically criticizing French lawyer Eric Andrieu and McDonald’s Vice President, Malcolm Hicks. I suggested that Malcolm Hick’s silence may have been motivated by the legal peril McDonald’s faced, rather than any strategy for resolution.

## Accusations of Continued Fraud and Inaction

I highlighted ongoing fraudulent activities allegedly occurring under McDonald’s operations in Europe, particularly in France and Germany. The lack of action to stop these purported crimes was contrasted sharply against Easterbrook’s public declarations in the media, where he claimed to be a “change agent for good,” which I called out as mere public relations tactics.

## Demands for Accountability and Legal Obligations

I reiterated demands for McDonald’s to address the unresolved issues from [the 2011 promotional game in France, which was a fraud](https://www.federal-bureau-of-investigation.com/open-letters/latest-crime-report-criminal-rico-case-targeting-mcdonalds#mcdonalds-france-monopoly-first-1-in-4-fraud-year-2011), asserting a quasi-contractual relationship that imposed legal obligations on McDonald’s to fulfill promised prizes. The urgency for McDonald’s to rectify these issues was stressed as a matter of both legal obligation and moral imperative.
